Featured work
The Singha sculpture

In March of 2025 I traveled to Thailand to spend a month with my dad. Everyday on our way home, we drove past Boon Rawd Brewery Park, a massive 3,400-acre eco-tourism destination in Chiang Rai. And in the front stood the distinctive 20 feet tall golden Singha logo dominating a landscape at the entrance. I started sketching and planning for my wooden singha sculpture. It always reminds me of the wonderful times I spent with my dad. I carved it from segmented walnut and maple blocks, and shaped it by hand in my garage. It's why the Singha is my mark.

The name

Loong Pete is what my friends' children call me. It's Thai for Uncle Pete. It's a name rooted in warmth, familiarity, and care, and that same spirit goes into every piece I make.

The Maker

About Pete

It started with a birdhouse and my daughters.

About five years ago, I built a birdhouse with my daughters and discovered something I didn't want to stop doing. Since then, woodworking has grown from curiosity into craft. I'm self-taught and always learning. I make anything from small boxes to larger statement pieces by hand, one build at a time.
